Health services across Jiwaka Province

Care and support for communities, families, and health facilities.

Jiwaka Provincial Health Authority supports healthcare services across Jiwaka Province, including rural health, hospital care, public health programs, outreach services, and referral support.

This page helps visitors understand the main healthcare service areas delivered and coordinated by JPHA.

Service Areas

  • Primary healthcare: Community-level and frontline care
  • Hospital services: Referral, inpatient, and emergency care
  • Public health: Prevention, awareness, and disease response
  • Outreach: Rural and remote healthcare support

Our Service Approach

JPHA works to improve healthcare access for people across Jiwaka Province. Its service approach connects hospitals, rural health centres, aid posts, public health programs, and community outreach.

The authority focuses on delivering practical, people-centered healthcare services that support families, patients, health workers, and communities.

  • Patient-focused: Helping people access the care they need.
  • Province-wide: Supporting all districts in Jiwaka Province.
  • Connected care: Linking rural health, hospital care, and public health.
  • Access-oriented: Supporting outreach and referral pathways.

Main Service Areas

Key healthcare services delivered and coordinated by JPHA.

Primary Care

Primary Healthcare

Supporting health centres, clinics, aid posts, basic treatment, preventive care, and community-based care close to where people live.

Hospital Services

Hospital and Referral Care

Supporting emergency services, inpatient care, referral pathways, diagnostics, and specialist healthcare through major referral facilities such as Kudjip Nazarene Hospital.

Public Health

Public Health Programs

Managing health promotion, disease prevention, immunization, health awareness, outbreak response, and community health programs.

Outreach

Rural and Remote Outreach

Extending healthcare services to hard-to-reach communities through outreach patrols, mobile clinics, and rural health support programs.

Family Health

Maternal, Child, and Family Health

Supporting pregnancy care, childbirth services, newborn care, child health, family wellbeing, and women’s health programs.

Medical Support

Medical Supply and Diagnostic Support

Supporting medicine distribution, medical supply coordination, laboratory services, and diagnostic support for health facilities.

Additional Support Areas

Other services that help strengthen healthcare delivery in Jiwaka Province.

Health Promotion and Awareness

Community health education, prevention messaging, and awareness activities that encourage early care-seeking.

Emergency and Referral Support

Helping patients access higher-level care through referral pathways and emergency response coordination.

Partner-Supported Programs

Working with government, churches, NGOs, and development partners to strengthen health services.

How People May Access Services

People may begin by visiting their nearest aid post, health centre, clinic, or main referral facility. Urgent cases should seek emergency help immediately.

  • Community-level care: Start with a local clinic, aid post, or health centre.
  • Referral care: Complex cases may be referred to larger facilities.
  • Public health support: Outreach and immunization may be delivered in communities.
  • Information access: Contact JPHA for service guidance and facility information.
