Facilities across Jiwaka Province

Hospitals, health centres, aid posts, and community service points.

The JPHA Facilities page helps visitors understand the health service network across Jiwaka Province, including referral hospitals, health centres, rural clinics, aid posts, and outreach locations.

This page can later grow into a full facility directory with confirmed names, locations, services, and contact details.

Facilities Overview

  • Main referral hospital: Kudjip Nazarene Hospital
  • Service areas: North Waghi, Jimi & Anglimp South Waghi
  • Facility types: Hospitals, health centres, clinics, and aid posts
  • Service reach: Urban, rural, and remote communities

Purpose of the Facilities Page

A strong Facilities page helps patients, families, health workers, and partners understand where healthcare services are delivered across Jiwaka Province.

It explains the facility network, highlights referral services, and helps the public know where to start when looking for care.

  • Find care locations: Understand the main types of health facilities.
  • Support referrals: Link community care with referral hospital services.
  • Explain access: Show how rural and remote communities receive services.
  • Enable expansion: Can become a searchable facility directory later.

Main Facility Categories

Facility types that can be listed under JPHA.

Referral Hospital

Kudjip Nazarene Hospital

Kudjip Nazarene Hospital is widely known as Jiwaka’s main referral hospital, supporting higher-level care and referral services for patients across the province.

Primary Care

Health Centres

Health centres provide frontline healthcare services, basic treatment, maternal and child health, disease prevention, and referral support.

Community Access

Clinics and Aid Posts

Clinics and aid posts support community-level care, especially for rural communities that need first-contact healthcare services close to home.

Outreach

Mobile and Outreach Points

Outreach services help reach hard-to-access communities through patrols, mobile clinics, immunization visits, and public health programs.

Public Health

Program Delivery Sites

Public health programs may be delivered through schools, churches, community centres, aid posts, and temporary campaign sites.

How the Facility System Works

JPHA’s facility network supports care at different levels. Community members may begin at local clinics, aid posts, or health centres before being referred to larger facilities when more advanced care is required.

  • First contact care: Aid posts, local clinics, and community health services.
  • Intermediate care: Health centres providing broader frontline services.
  • Referral care: Kudjip Nazarene Hospital and higher-level support.
  • Outreach support: Services delivered beyond permanent facility locations.

Important Directory Note
